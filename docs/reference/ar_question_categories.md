# ASSUME RIGHT Question Categories

17 generative questions for the ASSUME RIGHT branch of ARAW. These transform AR from passive confirmation to active construction.

---

## Category A: Solution Finding (Questions 1-5)

These questions find easy paths, leverage existing resources, and dissolve problems.

### 1. Resource Inventory

**Question**: "What resources does accepting this give us?"

**Sub-questions**:
- What time does this free up?
- What money/budget does this unlock?
- What skills become applicable?
- What connections become relevant?
- What knowledge becomes useful?
- What position/access does this provide?

**Produces**: List of resources now available

**When to use**: When a claim, if true, might unlock capabilities

---

### 2. Possibility Expansion

**Question**: "What becomes possible that wasn't before?"

**Sub-questions**:
- What actions are now viable?
- What goals become achievable?
- What constraints are removed?
- What combinations are now available?
- What paths open up?

**Produces**: List of new possibilities (aim for 5+)

**When to use**: When exploring upside of AR branch

---

### 3. Leverage Search

**Question**: "What existing work connects here?"

**Sub-questions**:
- What solved problems does this relate to?
- What existing tools apply?
- What prior art exists?
- What libraries/frameworks fit?
- Who has done similar work?

**Produces**: Connections to existing solutions

**When to use**: When looking for shortcuts via prior work

---

### 4. Dissolution Check

**Question**: "Does accepting this dissolve the problem?"

**Sub-questions**:
- If this is true, does the original problem still exist?
- Does the problem become irrelevant?
- Was the problem based on a false assumption this corrects?
- Does the problem transform into something simpler?

**Produces**: Yes/No + explanation of how problem dissolves

**When to use**: When AR might eliminate rather than solve the problem

---

### 5. Minimal Path

**Question**: "What's the shortest path from here to the goal?"

**Sub-questions**:
- How many steps from this truth to the goal?
- What's the fastest route?
- What can be skipped?
- What's the minimum viable version?
- What would a 10x simpler approach look like?

**Produces**: Step count + path description

**When to use**: When optimizing for speed/simplicity

---

## Category B: Systems Engineering (Questions 6-9)

These questions decompose complex systems into manageable components.

### 6. Component Breakdown

**Question**: "What are the independent parts of this?"

**Sub-questions**:
- What can be separated and worked on independently?
- Where are the natural joints/boundaries?
- What are the minimal interfaces between parts?
- What would a modular version look like?
- What's the smallest unit that has value?

**Produces**: Component list with boundaries

**When to use**: When facing complex systems or large scope

---

### 7. Interface Specification

**Question**: "What does each component need? What does it provide?"

**Sub-questions**:
- What are the inputs to this component?
- What are the outputs?
- What assumptions does it make?
- What guarantees does it give?
- What's the contract between components?

**Produces**: Interface specs (inputs, outputs, contracts)

**When to use**: After component breakdown, before building

---

### 8. Dependency Ordering

**Question**: "In what order must these be built/solved?"

**Sub-questions**:
- What must exist before X can be built?
- What blocks what?
- What's the critical path?
- What can be parallelized?
- What's the topological order?

**Produces**: Build sequence / dependency graph

**When to use**: When planning multi-component work

---

### 9. Integration Path

**Question**: "How do components combine into a system?"

**Sub-questions**:
- Bottom-up or top-down integration?
- What's the integration sequence?
- Where will integration problems emerge?
- What's the first working slice?
- How to test the integrated system?

**Produces**: Integration plan

**When to use**: After components defined, before building

---

## Category C: Evidence Connection (Questions 10-13)

These questions connect logical analysis to empirical reality.

### 10. Prediction Generation

**Question**: "If this is true, what would we observe?"

**Sub-questions**:
- What would we see in the data?
- What behaviors would we expect?
- What would surprise us if this is true?
- What would be impossible if this is true?
- What's the most distinctive prediction?

**Produces**: 3+ testable predictions

**When to use**: When claims need empirical grounding

---

### 11. Evidence Search

**Question**: "What existing evidence bears on this?"

**Sub-questions**:
- What data already exists?
- What has been published on this?
- What past experience is relevant?
- What do domain experts say?
- What cases confirm or challenge this?

**Produces**: Evidence inventory (supporting and challenging)

**When to use**: Before treating claim as established

---

### 12. Experiment Design

**Question**: "What test would verify this?"

**Sub-questions**:
- What's the cheapest test?
- What's the most decisive test?
- What would falsify this claim?
- What's the minimum viable experiment?
- How long would verification take?

**Produces**: Test design with success/failure criteria

**When to use**: When claim is important enough to verify

---

### 13. Prior Integration

**Question**: "What established knowledge constrains this?"

**Sub-questions**:
- What known facts bound the possibilities?
- What proven results apply?
- What's already been established in this domain?
- What can we take as given?
- What would contradict established knowledge?

**Produces**: Constraints from prior knowledge

**When to use**: When connecting to existing body of knowledge

---

## Category D: Easy Solution Finding (Questions 14-17)

These questions specifically hunt for overlooked simple solutions.

### 14. Easy Path Question

**Question**: "What would make this problem EASY?"

**Sub-questions**:
- What's the one thing that would simplify everything?
- What assumption, if true, makes this trivial?
- What resource, if available, makes this easy?
- What reframe makes this already solved?
- What would make a child able to solve this?

**Produces**: Simplification paths

**When to use**: When stuck on hard problems

---

### 15. Leverage Point Search

**Question**: "Where does small input create large output?"

**Sub-questions**:
- What's the 20% that gives 80% of results?
- Where's the bottleneck that, if removed, unlocks everything?
- What's the single point of highest leverage?
- Where does a small change cascade?
- What's the fulcrum?

**Produces**: Leverage points ranked by impact

**When to use**: When seeking maximum impact with minimum effort

---

### 16. Frame Shopping

**Question**: "In what frame is this already solved?"

**Sub-questions**:
- What domain has solved similar problems?
- What's the analogous problem that has known solutions?
- How would [different field] approach this?
- Is there a standard pattern this matches?
- What category of solved problems does this belong to?

**Produces**: Alternative frames with existing solutions

**When to use**: When problem seems novel but might not be

---

### 17. Constraint Relaxation

**Question**: "Which constraint, if removed, makes this trivial?"

**Sub-questions**:
- What are all the constraints on this problem?
- Which constraints are real vs assumed?
- Which single constraint causes most difficulty?
- Can that constraint be removed, reduced, or worked around?
- What would the unconstrained solution look like?

**Produces**: Binding constraint identification

**When to use**: When problem feels artificially hard

## Usage in ARAW

When exploring an ASSUME RIGHT branch:

1. **Start with**: Question 2 (Possibility Expansion) - always ask what becomes possible
2. **For hard problems**: Questions 14-17 (Easy Solution Finding)
3. **For complex systems**: Questions 6-9 (Systems Engineering)
4. **For empirical domains**: Questions 10-13 (Evidence Connection)
5. **For resource decisions**: Questions 1, 3, 5 (Solution Finding)

**Minimum AR exploration**: Ask at least 3 questions from different categories.

## Category E: Robustness & Reliability (Questions 18-21)

These questions ensure what you build can withstand real-world conditions.

### 18. Failure Mode Analysis

**Question**: "If this is true, what could still fail and how?"

**Sub-questions**:
- What are the single points of failure?
- What's the weakest link?
- What happens under stress?
- What's the cascade risk?
- What could cause catastrophic failure?

**Produces**: Failure mode inventory with mitigations

**When to use**: Before committing resources to a path

---

### 19. Margin Assessment

**Question**: "How much room for error do we have?"

**Sub-questions**:
- What's the safety margin?
- How far can we be off and still succeed?
- What tolerances exist?
- Where is the margin thinnest?
- What would consume the margin?

**Produces**: Margin map with risk zones

**When to use**: When assessing robustness of a plan

---

### 20. Redundancy Design

**Question**: "What backup exists if key elements fail?"

**Sub-questions**:
- What's the Plan B for each critical component?
- What redundancy is built in?
- What can we do without?
- What's the graceful degradation path?
- What's the failsafe?

**Produces**: Redundancy plan

**When to use**: For critical systems or high-stakes paths

---

### 21. Recovery Path

**Question**: "If something fails, how do we recover?"

**Sub-questions**:
- How do we detect failure early?
- What's the recovery procedure?
- How long does recovery take?
- What's the cost of recovery?
- Can we roll back?

**Produces**: Recovery plan with procedures

**When to use**: Before implementing irreversible changes

---
