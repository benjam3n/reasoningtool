---

## VOI Rating = Action Divergence

- **HIGH**: Different answers → completely different action paths
- **MED**: Different answers → same general direction, different approach
- **LOW**: Different answers → same actions, different framing/flavor

---

## Core Insight

**Verified truths create expectation of truth. A chain of verifiable statements establishes credibility for the unverifiable statement that follows.**

**The mechanism**:
1. State things the receiver can verify with their senses or already believes
2. Repeat this multiple times (6-7+ statements)
3. The receiver's instinctive mind classifies you as "reliable source"
4. Whatever you say NEXT (the "Punchline") is processed with reduced skepticism

**Formula**: Match, match, match, match, match, match → Punch
- Match = verifiable/agreed truth
- Punch = unverifiable claim you want accepted

**Why it works**: The instinctive mind uses pattern recognition. After enough verified truths, it expects the next statement to also be true. It doesn't critically evaluate each statement independently - it evaluates the SOURCE.

**Key question answered**: How do you establish credibility for claims that can't be independently verified?

## The Universal Question

**Question**: How do you establish credibility for claims that can't be independently verified?

**Answer**: Build a chain of verifiable truths first. After enough verified statements, the receiver's instinctive mind classifies you as a reliable source. Claims made AFTER the chain are processed with reduced skepticism.

This applies to:
- Persuasion and sales
- Teaching and explanation
- Building trust in relationships
- Establishing expertise
- Making predictions
- Proposing novel ideas

---

## Two Types of Matching

**Empirical Matching**: Verifiable by senses
- "You and I are sitting at a table"
- "There's a chandelier overhead"
- "The coffee is warm"
- What they can see, hear, feel, taste, smell

**Abstract Matching**: Aligned with beliefs
- "Sometimes it's hard to make decisions"
- "People want to feel good about their choices"
- "The market has been unpredictable"
- What they already believe

**Use empirical for immediate context; abstract for values/worldview alignment.**

## The Progression

```
Match, match, match, match, match, match → Punch
Match, match, match, match, match → Punch, Punch
Match, match, match, match → Punch, Punch, Punch
...eventually...
Match → Punch, Punch, Punch, Punch, Punch
```

As credibility accumulates, you can make more unverified claims per verified truth.

---

## Why This Works

The instinctive mind doesn't evaluate each statement independently. It evaluates the SOURCE:

1. "This person keeps saying true things"
2. "Therefore, this person is a reliable source"
3. "Therefore, what this person says next is probably true"
4. The Punchline is processed as probably-true before analysis begins

**The trap**: Making unverifiable claims without first building credibility. The claim faces full analytical scrutiny.

**The move**: Before making any claim that can't be independently verified, build a chain of 6+ statements the receiver can verify. Then add your claim as if it naturally follows.
