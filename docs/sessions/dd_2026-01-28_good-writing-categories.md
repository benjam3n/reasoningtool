Dimension Discovery: Criteria for High-Quality Writing                                                                                                                                
                                                                                                                                                                                        
  Step 1: Seed Examples                                                                                                                                                                 
                                                                                                                                                                                        
  Concrete examples of writing widely regarded as high quality:                                                                                                                         
                                                                                                                                                                                        
  1. George Orwell's essays ("Politics and the English Language")                                                                                                                       
  2. A well-crafted legal brief that wins a case                                                                                                                                        
  3. A clear, accurate API documentation page                                                                                                                                           
  4. A gripping opening chapter of a novel                                                                                                                                              
  5. A scientific paper that shifts a field's understanding                                                                                                                             
  6. A persuasive op-ed that changes public opinion                                                                                                                                     
  7. A personal letter that moves the recipient to tears                                                                                                                                
  8. A technical blog post that makes a complex topic accessible

  Step 4: Domain-Specific Dimensions                                                                                                                                                    
                                                                                                                                                                                        
  1. Clarity                                                                                                                                                                            
                                                                                                                                                                                        
  - What it captures: Can the reader understand what the writer means without unnecessary effort?                                                                                       
  - Values: Opaque, Ambiguous, Functional, Clear, Crystalline                                                                                                                           
  - Why it matters: The foundation. Without clarity, no other quality can land. Distinct from simplicity — complex ideas expressed clearly is the gold standard.                        
                                                                                                                                                                                        
  2. Precision                                                                                                                                                                          
                                                                                                                                                                                        
  - What it captures: Does every word earn its place? Are claims specific rather than vague?                                                                                            
  - Values: Vague/hand-wavy, Approximate, Adequate, Precise, Surgically exact                                                                                                           
  - Why it matters: Separates writing that gestures at ideas from writing that pins them down. Includes word choice, qualifier accuracy, and specificity of claims.                     
                                                                                                                                                                                        
  3. Structure                                                                                                                                                                          
                                                                                                                                                                                        
  - What it captures: How well the piece is organized — logical flow, transitions, pacing, hierarchy of ideas.                                                                          
  - Values: Disorganized, Loosely ordered, Competently structured, Tightly architected, Inevitably structured (reader feels each part had to come in that order)                        
  - Why it matters: Structure is the skeleton. Bad structure makes even good sentences fail because the reader can't follow the argument or narrative.                                  
                                                                                                                                                                                        
  4. Audience Calibration                                                                                                                                                               
                                                                                                                                                                                        
  - What it captures: Does the writing meet the reader where they are — in knowledge, vocabulary, expectations, and needs?                                                              
  - Values: Mismatched, Partially calibrated, Well-targeted, Precisely attuned                                                                                                          
  - Why it matters: Writing that talks over or under its audience fails regardless of other qualities. A brilliant paper that a lay reader can't parse isn't "good writing" for that    
  reader.                                                                                                                                                                               
                                                                                                                                                                                        
  5. Intellectual Honesty / Epistemic Rigor                                                                                                                                             
                                                                                                                                                                                        
  - What it captures: Does the writer represent reality accurately? Are counterarguments acknowledged? Are claims proportional to evidence?                                             
  - Values: Misleading, Selective, Fair, Rigorous, Unflinching                                                                                                                          
  - Why it matters: Writing that persuades through distortion may be effective but isn't good. This dimension separates propaganda from argumentation, hype from analysis.              
                                                                                                                                                                                        
  6. Voice / Authenticity                                                                                                                                                               
                                                                                                                                                                                        
  - What it captures: Does the writing sound like a specific human being thinking, or like a committee / template / algorithm?                                                          
  - Values: Generic/voiceless, Imitative, Competent-but-anonymous, Distinctive, Unmistakable                                                                                            
  - Why it matters: Voice is what makes writing memorable and trustworthy. It signals that a real mind is behind the words. Absence of voice is the most common marker of mediocre      
  writing.                                                                                                                                                                              
                                                                                                                                                                                        
  7. Economy                                                                                                                                                                            
                                                                                                                                                                                        
  - What it captures: Ratio of insight/value delivered to words spent. The opposite of bloat.                                                                                           
  - Values: Bloated, Padded, Adequate, Lean, Every-word-load-bearing                                                                                                                    
  - Why it matters: Respects the reader's time. Orwell's rule: "If it is possible to cut a word out, always cut it out." Distinct from brevity — a long piece can be economical if every
   paragraph advances the purpose.                                                                                                                                                      
                                                                                                                                                                                        
  8. Engagement / Pull                                                                                                                                                                  
                                                                                                                                                                                        
  - What it captures: Does the writing compel the reader to keep reading? Does it create forward momentum?                                                                              
  - Values: Repelling, Ignorable, Readable, Compelling, Unputdownable                                                                                                                   
  - Why it matters: Writing that is clear, precise, and honest but boring fails in practice. The reader stops reading. Mechanisms vary by genre (narrative tension, curiosity gaps,     
  rhythm, stakes, surprise).                                                                                                                                                            
                                                                                                                                                                                        
  9. Purpose Fulfillment                                                                                                                                                                
                                                                                                                                                                                        
  - What it captures: Does the writing accomplish what it set out to do? Does the reader walk away with what they needed?                                                               
  - Values: Fails its purpose, Partially delivers, Adequate, Fully delivers, Exceeds — reader gets more than expected                                                                   
  - Why it matters: This is the ultimate pragmatic test. A README that doesn't help you set up the project is bad writing, even if the prose is beautiful.                              
                                                                                                                                                                                        
  10. Coherence                                                                                                                                                                         
                                                                                                                                                                                        
  - What it captures: Do all parts serve the whole? Is there a unified argument, narrative, or throughline? Are there contradictions?                                                   
  - Values: Contradictory, Fragmented, Mostly consistent, Unified, Monolithic (single unbroken line of thought)                                                                         
  - Why it matters: Distinct from structure (which is about order). Coherence is about whether the parts agree with each other and build toward a single effect.

  Notes                                                                                                                                                                                 
                                                                                                                                                                                        
  - Genre-weighting matters: Not all dimensions carry equal weight in every context. API docs can have generic voice and still be excellent. A novel with no engagement is dead on      
  arrival regardless of other scores. The relative importance of each dimension shifts by genre and purpose.                                                                            
  - Minimum thresholds vs. excellence: Some dimensions are "hygiene factors" (clarity, coherence — must be above a floor) while others are "differentiators" (voice, engagement —       
  separate good from great).                                                                                                                                                            
  - The rigor dimension applies differently to fiction: For fiction, it maps to internal consistency, emotional truth, and plausibility rather than evidence-based claims.              
  - Economy is not brevity: A 500-page novel can be economical. A 200-word email can be bloated.
